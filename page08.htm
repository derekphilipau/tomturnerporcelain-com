TOM TURNER PORCELAIN
   
JUNE 12 FIRING

Pots that are high fired a second or third time are prone to crazing and bloating(small pimples). The perfect chemistry for Copper Red is prone to crazing, crackling, and if the red is present, you just don't see it. This crazing continues until the glaze and body are "at rest", neither pushing or pulling. The glaze crackles because it is shrinking more than the body. My suggestion is that after a year or so, completely cover the glazed area, not the unglazed foot, with a black magic marker. The ink goes into the crackle and remains after you rub off the magic marker. I use lacquer thinner to remove the ink, do not get the ink on unglazed porcelain. Ancient pots have dirt in the crackle from use, therefore, no need for ink. An Asian tea bowl would be colored by the tea. The small bloating is not wanted, but happens in the re-fire when a small air bubble heats and expands in a taffy-like body, think of blowing a bubble. At 2350 degrees F, the porcelain is soft like taffy. These red and white pots were supposed to be all red. Probably the greatest cause of frustration for the artist is expectations and realizations. Once I set aside my expectations and see the pots for what they are, I can see not a failure, but a beautiful pot in another way. I should also add that the crazing does not affect the pot in any way, nor will it cause future problems, and since the pot is vitreous, water does not seep through the crackle, as with low fired pots.
