TOM TURNER PORCELAIN
   
FEBRUARY 12 FIRING

These are my first attempts at Copper Red Oxblood in my new electric kiln. These are a bit of a rarity as Oxbloods are usually done in some sort of fuel burning kiln where a reduction atmosphere of carbon monoxide can be created to reduce the glaze. Copper Red Oxblood is one of the most difficult glazes to achieve in reduction, let alone oxidation. These are done with chemical reduction. I will continue with the Lunar Crystal and Oilspot glazes. Thanks for looking and please call or email with any questions.
